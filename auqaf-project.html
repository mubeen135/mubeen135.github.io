West Bengal Auqaf Board Website

Official Website Development for Statutory Government Body

Government Sector | Web Development | Public Portal

Project Overview

The West Bengal Auqaf Board is a statutory body established by the Government of West Bengal under the Waqf Act, 1995 (Central Act 43 of 1995). This project involved developing their official website to provide comprehensive information about the board's functions, governance structure, and administration of Waqf properties across the state.

Project Objective

To create a professional, user-friendly, and informative website that serves as the primary digital interface for the West Bengal Auqaf Board, facilitating transparency, accessibility, and efficient communication with the public.

About the Board

The West Bengal Auqaf Board oversees numerous Waqf properties across the state, including Mosques, Kabarsthan, Dargahs, and other registered Waqf properties. The Board functions with perpetual succession and a common seal, with the authority to acquire, hold, and manage properties in accordance with the law.

Governance Structure

The Board is constituted in accordance with Section 14 of the Waqf Act, 1995, with both elected and nominated members. The Chairperson is elected from amongst its members, currently held by a retired Justice of the Hon'ble High Court, Calcutta.

Administration

The State Government appoints a Chief Executive Officer (CEO) in consultation with the Board. All employees are considered public servants under Section 21 of the Indian Penal Code, ensuring accountability and transparency in operations.

Key Functions of the Board

Function Category Description
Superintendence General superintendence of all Waqf estates and properties in West Bengal
Administration Ensuring proper maintenance and lawful use of Waqf incomes and resources
Accountability Monitoring and auditing Waqf finances with transparency
Heritage Protection Protecting religious and cultural heritage linked to Waqf properties

Central Waqf Council (CWC)

The Central Waqf Council (CWC), established by the Government of India in 1964, serves as an advisory body on matters related to the functioning of State Auqaf Boards and administration of Waqf properties across the country. The CWC works closely with State Auqaf Boards to ensure proper administration and management in compliance with the Waqf Act, 1995.
